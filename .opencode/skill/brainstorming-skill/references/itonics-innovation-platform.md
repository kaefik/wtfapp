# ITONICS Innovation Platform: ChatGPT Prompts for Ideation

**Source URL**: <https://www.itonics-innovation.com/blog/chatgpt-prompts-for-ideation>

**Total Prompts Documented**: 79 ChatGPT prompts organized across 8 pattern categories

---

## Overview

This reference documents ChatGPT prompts designed to facilitate organizational ideation and innovation workshops. The prompts serve as templates that organizations can customize with their specific contexts, industries, products, personas, and challenges. All prompts use bracketed placeholders `[like this]` to indicate where customization should occur.

**Key Principle**: ChatGPT functions as a "virtual member" of brainstorming teams, augmenting rather than replacing human expertise and organizational knowledge.

## Pattern Categories and Prompts

### 1. Disruptive Thinking (13 Prompts)

Prompts designed to challenge fundamental assumptions, reimagine business models, and explore radical departures from current practices.

#### 1.1 Foundational Disruption

1. **Industry from Scratch**

   - "If we were to start our industry from scratch today, with all the modern tools and technologies available, what would it look like?"
   - _Usage_: Identifies which current practices are legacy constraints vs. value-creating activities

2. **Cross-Industry Leadership**

   - "How would our business operate if it were run by leaders from a completely unrelated industry (e.g., aerospace, fashion, or gaming)?"
   - _Usage_: Brings external mental models into internal problem-solving

3. **Constraint-Driven Innovation**

   - "If our budget were cut by 80% but we still had to meet our objectives, how would we do it?"
   - _Usage_: Forces prioritization and reveals inefficiencies

4. **Core Product Loss**
   - "What if our primary product/service no longer existed? How would we adapt and still serve our customers?"
   - _Usage_: Separates customer needs from current delivery mechanisms

#### 1.2 Assumption Reversal

5. **Assumption Inversion**

   - "List all the assumptions about our industry. Now, what would it look like if the opposite of each assumption were true?"
   - _Usage_: Systematically generates alternative business models

6. **Target Audience Shift**
   - "If our primary target audience suddenly became [choose a different demographic], how would our product/service change?"
   - _Usage_: Explores market expansion and repositioning opportunities

#### 1.3 Functional Reduction and Historical Exploration

7. **Single Function Focus**

   - "What if our product/service could only perform one primary function? Which one would it be, and how would it transform its use?"
   - _Usage_: Identifies core value and eliminates feature bloat

8. **Historical Practice Revival**
   - "How did companies in our industry operate 50 or 100 years ago? Are there forgotten practices or tools that could be reinvented with today's technology?"
   - _Usage_: Discovers overlooked proven practices that could modernize

#### 1.4 Future-Focused Disruption

9. **Future Challenge Anticipation**

   - "It's the year 2050. What are the potential challenges our industry will face, and how can we preemptively address them now?"
   - _Usage_: Builds future-proof strategies and identifies emerging needs

10. **Technology Integration**

    - "How would our business change if we integrated the latest [AI, AR, VR, blockchain, etc.] technologies into our core offerings?"
    - _Usage_: Explores technology-enabled business model transformation

11. **Sustainability Mandate**
    - "How would we operate if we were mandated to become a zero-waste or carbon-neutral company within a year?"
    - _Usage_: Identifies sustainability-driven innovation opportunities

#### 1.5 Comparative and Collaborative Approaches

12. **Global Practice Adoption**

    - "How do similar companies operate in other parts of the world? What practices or concepts can we adopt and adapt?"
    - _Usage_: Leverages global business intelligence for local adaptation

13. **Cross-Industry Collaboration**
    - "If we were to collaborate with a company outside our industry, what might our joint product/service look like?"
    - _Usage_: Generates ecosystem and partnership opportunities

### 2. Rapid Idea Generation (6 Prompts)

Prompts designed for high-velocity ideation, quick prototyping of concepts, and mass idea generation under time constraints.

1. **Time-Constrained Brainstorm**

   - "You have 60 seconds. Jot down as many solutions as possible to [specific problem]."
   - _Usage_: Overcomes analysis paralysis by forcing rapid output
   - _Suitable for_: Initial ideation phases, warm-up exercises

2. **Batch Innovation**

   - "Generate 5 innovative ideas for [specific topic]."
   - _Usage_: Creates multiple solution vectors quickly
   - _Suitable for_: Challenge definitions, proof-of-concept generation

3. **Persona-Specific Innovation**

   - "Propose three product ideas tailored for [persona and use case, e.g., remote workers in cold climates]."
   - _Usage_: Grounds ideas in specific user contexts
   - _Suitable for_: User-centered innovation, market segmentation

4. **Feature Enhancement**

   - "Given a [product], how can we add three unique features to it, making it indispensable for [persona]?"
   - _Usage_: Targets feature prioritization for specific user segments
   - _Suitable for_: Product roadmapping, competitive differentiation

5. **Technology Application**

   - "How can we leverage [specific technology] in the [specific industry] for a unique customer experience?"
   - _Usage_: Matches emerging technologies to industry-specific applications
   - _Suitable for_: Technology adoption strategy, innovation sourcing

6. **Scenario-Based Models**
   - "Imagine a world where [specific scenario]. Propose business models that would thrive in this environment."
   - _Usage_: Generates future-fit business models
   - _Suitable for_: Strategic foresight, scenario planning

### 3. Innovation/Ideation Workshops (10 Prompts)

Prompts designed for structured workshop facilitation, concept development, and multi-day innovation programs.

1. **Future Persona Needs**

   - "Imagine a specific customer persona [define persona] using our product/service in 2030. What are their needs, challenges, and desires?"
   - _Usage_: Extends personas forward in time
   - _Workshop phase_: Discovery and empathy mapping

2. **Future Market Conditions**

   - "Describe a world in 2035 where renewable energy sources are the only power options. What new products or services are in demand?"
   - _Usage_: Identifies market opportunities created by systemic change
   - _Workshop phase_: Market analysis and trend forecasting

3. **Concept Expansion**

   - "List and explain five unique functions a futuristic 'smart wallet' could have beyond just holding money."
   - _Usage_: Extends existing concept boundaries with future vision
   - _Workshop phase_: Feature ideation and concept expansion

4. **Concept Merging**

   - "Merge the concepts of [Concept A] and [Concept B]. What innovative applications might emerge?"
   - _Usage_: Generates novel combinations through systematic mixing
   - _Workshop phase_: Creative synthesis

5. **Experience Journey Mapping**

   - "Outline a user's experience journey when first interacting with a virtual shopping assistant powered by AI."
   - _Usage_: Maps touchpoints and moment-of-truth interactions
   - _Workshop phase_: UX design and service design

6. **Company Collaboration**

   - "If Company X and Company Y collaborated, what innovative product might result?"
   - _Usage_: Explores partnership synergies and ecosystem opportunities
   - _Workshop phase_: Strategic partnership exploration

7. **Values-Aligned Innovation**

   - "Propose a business model for [industry/brand] that ensures ethical sourcing, environmental responsibility, and community empowerment."
   - _Usage_: Generates sustainability-linked business models
   - _Workshop phase_: Purpose-driven innovation

8. **Competitive Response**

   - "If you were a competitor to our company, what revolutionary product or service would you introduce to challenge our market dominance?"
   - _Usage_: Identifies competitive vulnerabilities and emerging threats
   - _Workshop phase_: Competitive analysis and defensive strategy

9. **Competitive Perspective**

   - "If we were our main competitor, how would we approach [describe challenge] differently?"
   - _Usage_: Generates alternative problem-solving approaches
   - _Workshop phase_: Perspective-taking and strategy diversification

10. **Physical Prototyping**
    - "Using only the materials on your table [list materials], create a physical prototype of a solution to [specific problem]."
    - _Usage_: Forces tangible concept development with constraints
    - _Workshop phase_: Rapid prototyping and constraint-based design

### 4. Diversified Brainstorming (8 Prompts)

Prompts designed to introduce multiple perspectives, break cognitive patterns, and ensure inclusive ideation across different viewpoints.

1. **Multi-Age Perspective**

   - "Describe this challenge from the viewpoint of a 10-year-old, an 80-year-old, and a non-human entity, such as a robot."
   - _Usage_: Breaks generational bias and introduces non-human reasoning
   - _Perspective variety_: Cognitive development stage, life experience, computational logic

2. **Cross-Industry Analogy**

   - "If our company was operating in [different industry], how might our approach to this problem differ?"
   - _Usage_: Applies best practices from adjacent industries
   - _Suitable for_: Innovation scouting from other sectors

3. **Cross-Cultural Adaptation**

   - "How might this idea be perceived or adapted differently in cultures across North America, Africa, and Asia?"
   - _Usage_: Identifies localization requirements and cultural variations
   - _Suitable for_: Global product strategy, cultural sensitivity analysis

4. **Long-Term Trend Impact**

   - "Fast forward to 2050. How have global trends and societal changes impacted our product/service?"
   - _Usage_: Tests idea resilience across multi-decade timeframes
   - _Suitable for_: Legacy planning, future-proofing strategies

5. **Stakeholder Role Reversal**

   - "Imagine our customers are now the product designers and we're the end-users. What changes might they suggest?"
   - _Usage_: Reveals customer frustrations and unmet needs through design choices
   - _Suitable for_: Customer co-creation, empathy building

6. **Usage Constraint Design**

   - "What if our users could only interact with our product for 30 seconds a day? How would that change its design and functionality?"
   - _Usage_: Forces extreme usability and focuses on essential features
   - _Suitable for_: Feature prioritization, minimalist design thinking

7. **Inverse Brainstorming**

   - "List all the ways we shouldn't solve this problem. How can these 'bad' ideas inspire unconventional good ones?"
   - _Usage_: Generates contrarian solutions by exploring failure modes
   - _Suitable for_: Risk identification and novel problem-solving

8. **Risk-to-Opportunity Conversion**
   - "Identify the riskiest aspect of our current idea. Now, brainstorm ways to turn that risk into a unique selling point."
   - _Usage_: Converts competitive weaknesses into differentiators
   - _Suitable for_: Risk management, competitive positioning

### 6. Idea Challenge Framing (10 Prompts)

Prompts designed to reframe problems, contextualize challenges, and define innovation briefs for organizational innovation programs and contests.

1. **Challenge Naming**

   - "Provide suggestions for an engaging title for an ideation contest that addresses [describe challenge]."
   - _Usage_: Creates compelling problem statements for internal/external innovation programs
   - _Application_: Crowdsourcing setup, innovation competition framing

2. **Challenge Contextualization**

   - "Provide a brief history of [describe challenge]. How have companies or industries faced similar problems?"
   - _Usage_: Establishes historical context and reveals precedent solutions
   - _Application_: Stakeholder alignment, prior art identification

3. **Stakeholder Definition**

   - "From the perspective of a key stakeholder, how would they define the core of this challenge?"
   - _Usage_: Surfaces multiple stakeholder interpretations of the problem
   - _Application_: Needs assessment, perspective alignment

4. **Opportunity Reframing**

   - "If we considered this challenge not as a problem but as an opportunity, how might we redefine it?"
   - _Usage_: Shifts mindset from problem-solving to opportunity-seeking
   - _Application_: Positive framing, asset-based problem definition

5. **Future Evolution**

   - "Project this challenge five years into the future. How does it evolve, and what new dimensions might emerge?"
   - _Usage_: Identifies emerging aspects of the challenge
   - _Application_: Future-proofing solutions, long-term strategy

6. **Success Outcomes**

   - "If we address this challenge successfully, what are the top three positive outcomes we expect?"
   - _Usage_: Defines success criteria and impact vision
   - _Application_: Goal setting, outcome measurement

7. **Societal Impact Framing**

   - "Frame the challenge in terms of its wider implications on society or the environment."
   - _Usage_: Elevates challenge importance and connects to purpose
   - _Application_: Stakeholder engagement, mission-driven innovation

8. **Risk-Management Framing**

   - "From a risk-management viewpoint, how might we frame this challenge?"
   - _Usage_: Identifies risks embedded in the challenge
   - _Application_: Risk assessment, mitigation strategy development

9. **Realistic Ambition Balance**

   - "Given our current resources, how should we frame this challenge to be both ambitious and achievable?"
   - _Usage_: Calibrates challenge difficulty to organizational capacity
   - _Application_: Resource planning, scope definition

10. **Success Metrics Definition**
    - "If we were to address this challenge, how would we measure success? What KPIs are most relevant?"
    - _Usage_: Establishes measurement frameworks and performance indicators
    - _Application_: Evaluation planning, outcomes tracking

## Implementation Guidance

### Customization Pattern

All 79 prompts use bracketed placeholders to enable customization:

- `[specific problem]` - Insert your organization's current challenge
- `[specific topic]` - Insert your product category, service type, or innovation domain
- `[persona]` - Insert your target user archetype or customer segment
- `[specific product]` - Insert your actual product or service name
- `[specific technology]` - Insert emerging technology (AI, AR, VR, blockchain, etc.)
- `[specific industry]` - Insert your industry or adjacent industries
- `[Concept A/B]` - Insert related concepts to merge
- `[specific trend]` - Insert market trends relevant to your domain
- `[specific scenario]` - Insert future-state assumptions
- `[specific event]` - Insert potential market-moving events

### Workshop Application

**Rapid Idea Generation** (30-60 minutes): Use prompts 2 and 6 in Category 2

- Generates 15-30 ideas quickly
- Suitable for: Problem warm-up, ideation kickoff

**Half-Day Workshop** (4 hours): Combine prompts from Categories 2, 3, 4, 5

- Structured progression: rapid generation → workshop facilitation → diversified perspectives → concept refinement
- Suitable for: Cross-functional innovation sessions, product development

**Full-Day Innovation Program** (8 hours): Use prompts systematically across all categories

- Morning: Disruptive thinking (Category 1) + trend synthesis (Category 7)
- Midday: Rapid generation (Category 2) + workshop facilitation (Category 3)
- Afternoon: Diversified brainstorming (Category 4) + concept enhancement (Category 5)
- Late afternoon: Scenario exploration (Category 8) + challenge framing (Category 6)
- Suitable for: Strategic innovation programs, multi-day innovation challenges

**Ongoing Challenge Management**: Use Category 6 (Idea Challenge Framing) to set up innovation contests

- Establishes clear problem definitions
- Frames challenges for internal/external crowds
- Defines success criteria and measurement

## Core Principles from Source

1. **ChatGPT as Team Member**: Prompts are designed for ChatGPT to function as a "virtual member" of the brainstorming team, not as a replacement for human judgment or organizational expertise.

2. **Placeholder Customization**: All prompts expect customization with organizational context, specific products/services, industry knowledge, and current challenges.

3. **Structured Ideation**: The eight categories progress from radical rethinking → rapid generation → workshop structure → perspective diversity → concept refinement → challenge definition → trend analysis → future scenario building.

4. **Multiple Time Horizons**: Prompts reference multiple future timeframes (1-2 years, 5 years, 10 years, 2035-2050) to support both near-term tactics and long-term strategy.

5. **Stakeholder Inclusivity**: Several prompts explicitly introduce diverse perspectives (ages, cultures, roles, industries) to reduce groupthink and cognitive bias.
